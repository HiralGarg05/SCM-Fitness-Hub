PUSH-UPS

Push-ups are a fundamental bodyweight exercise that engages multiple muscle groups, primarily targeting the upper body and core. They’re versatile, effective, and can be performed anywhere with no equipment required. Here’s a breakdown of the key aspects of push-ups:
How to Perform a Standard Push-Up
 Starting Position: Begin in a plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, fingers pointing forward, and your body forming a straight line from head to heels. Lowering Phase: Slowly bend your elbows, keeping them close to your body or at a slight outward angle (not flared out). Lower your chest toward the ground while maintaining a straight line in your body. Bottom Position: Stop when your chest is just above the ground, or when your elbows reach a 90-degree angle. Pushing Phase: Press through your palms to extend your elbows, pushing your body back up to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

SQUATS

Squats are one of the most effective exercises for building lower body strength, stability, and muscle. They engage multiple muscles and joints, making them ideal for improving functional fitness and enhancing athletic performance.
How to Perform a Basic Squat

Starting Position: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing slightly outward. Hold your chest up, and engage your core for balance.
Lowering Phase: Begin the movement by pushing your hips back as if you’re sitting down into a chair. Bend your knees and lower your body until your thighs are parallel (or slightly below parallel) to the floor.
Bottom Position: Keep your knees in line with your toes and your weight centered over your heels. Make sure your back stays straight and avoid rounding your shoulders forward.
Rising Phase: Drive through your heels to return to a standing position, straightening your legs and engaging your glutes as you rise. vRepeat for the desired number of repetitions.

LUNGES

Lunges are a highly effective lower-body exercise that primarily targets the legs and glutes. They’re versatile, easy to modify, and help build balance and stability, making them ideal for a variety of fitness goals.
How to Perform a Basic Lunge

Starting Position: Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart, shoulders relaxed, and hands at your sides or on your hips for balance.
Step Forward: Take a big step forward with your right foot, keeping your torso upright and core engaged.
Lowering Phase: Bend both knees to lower your body until your back knee nearly touches the ground and your front thigh is parallel to the floor.
Bottom Position: Keep your front knee directly above your ankle, not letting it move past your toes. Your back knee should hover slightly above the ground.
>Return to Start: Push through the heel of your front foot to return to the starting position.
Repeat for the desired reps, alternating legs or performing all reps on one side before switching.

BENCH-PRESS

Bench Press is one of the most popular and effective exercises for developing upper body strength, particularly targeting the chest, shoulders, and triceps. Often performed with a barbell or dumbbells, the bench press is a staple in strength training and bodybuilding routines.
How to Perform a Standard Barbell Bench Press
Set Up: Lie flat on a bench with your feet firmly on the floor and your back pressed into the bench. Position the barbell above your chest at arm's length, with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
Grip:b Grip the bar firmly, keeping your wrists straight and aligned with your forearms. Your palms should face forward.
Lowering Phase:Slowly lower the bar down toward the mid-chest, keeping your elbows at about a 45-degree angle from your torso to protect the shoulders.
Bottom Position: Stop when the bar is just above your chest or lightly touches it, keeping your forearms perpendicular to the ground.
Pressing Phase: Push the bar back up to the starting position by extending your arms, pressing through your chest and triceps.
Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, keeping your form steady throughout.

CARDIO

Cardio short for cardiovascular exercise, refers to any physical activity that increases your heart rate and engages large muscle groups to improve the efficiency of your heart, lungs, and circulatory system. Cardio exercises are primarily aimed at improving cardiovascular endurance and overall fitness, and they are highly effective for burning calories and improving heart health.
How Often Should You Do Cardio?

General Guidelines: The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week.
For Weight Loss: If weight loss is a goal, you may want to aim for higher cardio volumes, such as 30-60 minutes most days of the week, especially when combined with a proper diet.
For General Fitness: 3-5 days a week of moderate-intensity cardio is sufficient for maintaining good cardiovascular health.

STEP-UPS

Step-ups are a simple yet effective lower body exercise that targets the legs, glutes, and core. Step-ups are often included in strength training routines for their ability to improve functional strength, balance, and stability. They can be done with just body weight or with added resistance, such as dumbbells or a barbell, to increase intensity. Step-ups mimic everyday movements like climbing stairs, making them excellent for improving mobility and strength for daily activities.
How to Perform a Step-up

Starting Position: Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart in front of a sturdy platform or bench. You can use a step, bench, or any raised surface that's about knee-height or slightly lower.
Step Up: Place your right foot firmly on the platform. Push through the heel of your right foot to lift your body onto the platform. Your left leg will remain on the floor, and your right leg will be fully extended at the top.
Bring the Left Leg Up (Optional): To increase the challenge, bring your left knee up toward your chest once your right leg is fully on the platform. This adds a bit of balance and core work.
Step Down: Slowly lower your body back down to the starting position by bending your right knee and stepping your left foot back onto the floor.
Repeat: Perform the desired number of reps on one leg before switching to the other leg.

CRUNCHES

Crunches are a popular core exercise primarily aimed at strengthening the abdominal muscles. They are an excellent exercise for toning the abs and improving overall core stability. Crunches specifically target the rectus abdominis (the "six-pack" muscles) and the obliques, helping to improve posture and provide support for the spine.
How to Perform a Basic Crunch

Starting Position: Lie flat on your back on a mat with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Place your hands lightly behind your head (or crossed over your chest, depending on preference). Keep your elbows wide to avoid pulling on your neck.
Engage the Core: Tighten your abdominal muscles and press your lower back into the floor. This will activate your core and prepare your body for the movement.
Lift Your Upper Body: Slowly curl your upper body towards your knees by lifting your shoulders and head off the ground. Keep your lower back pressed into the mat. The movement should come from your abs, not from your neck or arms.
Top of the Movement: At the peak, your upper back should be slightly off the floor, with your shoulder blades lifted, and your chin slightly tucked in to prevent straining your neck.
Lower Back Down: Slowly lower your upper body back to the starting position with control, making sure not to let your head or neck jerk forward.
