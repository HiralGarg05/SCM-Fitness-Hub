Cycling

Cycling is a versatile and enjoyable cardiovascular workout that can be performed outdoors or indoors on a stationary bike. It is low-impact, making it suitable for people of all fitness levels, and offers numerous health benefits, from improving heart health to building lower-body strength.
How to Perform Cycling:
1.Choose a bike that fits your size and comfort level.
2. Wear appropriate safety gear, including a helmet.
3.Adjust your seat and handlebars for proper posture.
4.Start pedaling at a steady pace and gradually increase speed or resistance.

Rowing

Rowing Rowing is a low-impact exercise that provides an excellent cardiovascular workout while strengthening both the upper and lower body. It can be performed on a rowing machine indoors or on water using a boat and oars. Rowing mimics the motion of rowing a boat, engaging multiple muscle groups simultaneously.
How to Perform Rowing

Start Position (The Catch):
1.Sit on the seat and strap your feet securely to the footrests.
2.Grip the handle with both hands, keeping your arms extended and back straight.
3.Bend your knees and slide forward, keeping the shins vertical.
The Drive:
1.Push through your legs to extend them fully while maintaining a straight back.
2.Pull the handle toward your chest, just below the ribs, as your legs straighten.
The Finish:
Lean back slightly, engaging your core, and complete the pull.
The Recovery:
Reverse the motion by extending your arms, leaning forward, and bending your knees to return to the starting position.

High Kness High knees are a dynamic exercise used in warm-ups, cardio workouts, or as part of high-intensity interval training (HIIT). The movement involves running in place with exaggerated knee lifts, engaging the lower body, core, and cardiovascular system.

Running

Running is a fundamental and dynamic form of aerobic exercise that involves propelling the body forward by repeatedly moving one leg at a time while maintaining both feet off the ground during certain phases of the stride. It is a natural and effective way to improve cardiovascular health, build endurance, and strengthen various muscle groups.
How to Perform Running

Movement:A continuous cycle of alternating steps where each foot strikes the ground momentarily before lifting off again. Unlike walking, running involves a "flight phase," where both feet are off the ground simultaneously.
Posture:Keep your body upright or slightly leaning forward. Relax your shoulders and engage your core for stability.
Arm Action:Arms should be bent at a 90-degree angle, swinging naturally in coordination with leg movements to help maintain rhythm and balance.
Stride:The length and frequency of your steps vary depending on speed, terrain, and individual biomechanics.
Gait:Running can differ in style (e.g., heel-to-toe, midfoot, or forefoot strike) based on the runner's preference and biomechanics.

Burpes

Burpesare a full-body, high-intensity exercise that combines strength and cardio training. This movement is a staple in many fitness routines due to its effectiveness in building endurance, improving agility, and strengthening multiple muscle groups simultaneously.

How to Perform Burpes

Starting Position:Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, arms at your sides.
Squat Down:Lower your body into a squat position, placing your hands flat on the ground in front of you.
Kick Back:Jump your feet backward to land in a plank position. Ensure your body forms a straight line from head to heels, with your core engaged.
Push-Up (Optional):Lower your chest to the ground for a push-up, then push back up into the plank position. This step can be skipped for a beginner modification.
Return to Squat:Jump your feet forward to return to the squat position.
Jump Up:Explosively jump into the air from the squat, reaching your arms overhead.
Repeat:Land softly and immediately move into the next repetition.

Lunges With Jumping

Lunges With Jumping are a versatile and effective lower-body exercise that targets multiple muscle groups while improving balance, coordination, and flexibility. They are commonly used in strength training, rehabilitation, and functional fitness routines.
How to perform lunges with jumping

Starting Position:Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart, arms at your sides or on your hips for balance.
Step Forward:Take a step forward with one leg, keeping your torso upright and core engaged.
Lower Your Body:Bend both knees to lower your body until your front thigh is parallel to the ground and your back knee hovers just above the floor. Ensure the front knee is directly above the ankle and not extending past your toes.
Return to Start:Push through the heel of your front foot to return to the starting position. Repeat with the other leg.

Treadmill Running

Treadmill Running is a form of indoor running performed on a treadmill, a machine designed with a moving belt that simulates the action of running. It is a popular exercise for cardiovascular fitness, weight management, and endurance training, offering the convenience of a controlled environment.
How to Perform Treadmill Running

Start Slowly:Begin at a walking or slow jogging pace to warm up.
Adjust Settings:Increase speed or incline gradually to suit your fitness level and training goals.
Maintain Form:Run upright with a natural gait, keeping your head up and arms swinging naturally.
Cool Down:Gradually reduce speed toward the end to transition into a cooldown phase.

Mountain Climbers are a high-intensity, full-body exercise that combines cardio and strength training. How to Perform Mountain Climbers

Starting Position:Begin in a high plank position with your hands directly under your shoulders, arms extended, and body forming a straight line from head to heels.Engage your core to keep your back flat and hips level.
